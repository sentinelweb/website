1. What is process?

In software when we refer to process we refer to workflow. Software projects (just like most other projects) are broken up into smaller units of work. There are many ways - but at the moment a lot of teams focus on features, which are a "A distinguishing characteristic of a software item". Features can then be broken up into epics and the epics further broken down into tasks. A task is something that can usually be be completed in a sprint - which is generally a 1 or 2 week work cycle where something is added to the software.

Where process comes in is how to define these features & tasks - and also how to deliver them. The best process is one that makes sure that everyone working together on a task knows what needs to be done with no misunderstandings. The main things that go wrong with software development generally come down to problems with defining what needs to be done and when ... there can be misunderstandings with what the business wants,Ã¥ and the business can also misunderstand what their customers want.

2. Understanding requirements

Before starting to build, it's important to make sure every stakeholder knows what the objective is - it sounds really simple, but in 25 years of software development - most of the delays I have seen are simply down to this - either misunderstanding customer requirements and having to change requirements later (which is harder than building it right the first time) or misunderstandings in what has been specified.

So what are the ways to avoid these problems?

2.1 Working with customers

Before starting to write any software a lot of time can be saved by thinks like:

  • Conducting customer interviews : Asking non-leading questions and seeing what ideas the customer has.
  • Paper prototyping : Simply drawing a UI on a piece of paper and seeing if someone can figure out what to do - and if they like it.
  • Landing pages : Making a fake homepage using your tagline and USP. You can advertise this and see what the response is from the public by seeing if they sign up for a newsletter or click a CTA button.
Doing these simple things can help to give you the best idea of what you need - and can even change the direction of a project at an early stage - saving valuable time.

2.2 Making a plan

Once we have the requirements in place, we need to identify the features we want to build and prioritise them. Then break them down into smaller features and tasks and order them so that they can be delivered incrementally. This is where it is best to work with experienced developers - as it is very easy to underestimate what needs to be done and incur more time, costs and bugs.

3 Build and Refine

The goal is to get something to market as soon a possible - this allows a process of continuous improvement to take place, where users, beta testers, and other stakeholders can provide feedback so that the most important things get done first and no time is wasted on things that people don't use.

Conclusion

So we can see that it is best to do sufficient Product development before doing any Software development. This saves much valuable time and makes the difference between success and failure in app development. Doing the simple things makes all the difference before diving into the harder tasks. Companies such as Trainline have got these things right, and it shows in their ability to constantly deliver high quality and useful functionality to customers.
